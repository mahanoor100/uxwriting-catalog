Project Brief
The Problem
Students in Africa are earning, spending, and losing money without a system.

University students across Sub-Saharan Africa receive stipends, allowances, MPESA transfers, and part-time income — often all at once. With no structured way to track spending, most run out of money before the month ends.

The Solution
Pesa — A budgeting companion that speaks student.

Pesa is a lightweight mobile app that helps students set budgets, log daily expenses, split costs with roommates, and build small savings goals — all in under 30 seconds per interaction. No banking jargon. No shame. Just clarity.

Target Users
👩🏾‍🎓
Amara, 20
University of Ghana, Accra. Receives a monthly allowance from parents via MPESA. Shares rent with 3 roommates. First time managing her own finances.
👨🏿‍💻
Tunde, 22
University of Lagos. Freelances for pocket money. Gets paid irregularly. Wants to save for a new laptop but always finds the money gone by month-end.
👩🏽‍🏫
Zawadi, 21
Strathmore University, Nairobi. Has a part-time job. Confident with money but needs a system to track where it goes. Budget-conscious but not a spreadsheet person.
Product Goals
1
Core Goal
Help students finish the month with money to spare
Pesa's #1 job is to make budget remaining visible at a glance — not buried in graphs. Users should know in under 3 seconds how much they have left this week.
2
Engagement Goal
Build a daily logging habit in 14 days
Research shows that users who log expenses for 14 consecutive days stay with a budgeting app. Every notification, nudge, and celebration is written to reinforce this loop.
3
Cultural Goal
Feel African, not translated
Copy references local realities: MPESA transfers, "sending money home," eating out vs. cooking in the hostel. No Silicon Valley assumptions.
4
Trust Goal
Never make users feel judged for their spending
Budget overruns are framed as information, not failure. The app is a non-judgmental advisor — firm when needed, always supportive.

Brand Foundation
Voice & Tone

Pesa's voice is a financially-savvy older sibling — warm, direct, never condescending. It doesn't lecture. It gives you the truth with a little warmth on top.

Voice Pillars
🌱
Real, Not Corporate
We write like a smart friend, not a bank. Short sentences. Plain words. No jargon students have to Google.
"You've got KES 1,200 left this week.
That's ₦1,200 more than last week — nice."
🎯
Direct, Not Blunt
We say what needs to be said. We don't bury the lead or pad with pleasantries. But we always soften facts with warmth.
"You've spent 80% of your food budget
with 12 days to go. Time to cook more?"
🤝
Empowering, Not Shaming
We never make users feel bad about how they spend. We frame overspending as information, not failure.
"Rent went over budget this month.
Let's plan better for next month."
✨
Celebratory, Not Excessive
We celebrate wins — but we match the energy. A small win gets a quiet nod. A big goal gets a proper cheer.
"Goal reached. Your ₦30,000 laptop
fund is full. Go get it. 🎉"
Tone Adaptation by Context
The voice stays the same. The tone shifts based on what the user is feeling in that moment.

→ Use "spending"

→ Use "not enough in your budget"

→ Use "use"

→ Use "set aside" or "budgeted"

Errors & Alerts

Error messages are the most read copy in any app. Users who hit an error are already frustrated. Copy must acknowledge, explain simply, and give one clear next step. Never blame the user.

Error State Cards
📵
No Internet
You're offline. Check your data or wifi — your budgets are still saved locally.
⏳
Taking too long
That's taking longer than usual. Might be on our side. Try again in a moment.
🚫
Session expired
You've been logged out for security. Sign back in to continue.
⚠️
Key principle: Never say "Something went wrong" without a next step. Always give the user one clear action to take. Never blame the user — even if the error is their fault.

UX Writer's Checklist

Run every Pesa screen through this checklist before shipping. Click to mark items done.

Clarity & Language
  • ✓
    Every sentence is under 20 words
  • ✓
    No jargon — every financial term is either plain or explained
  • ✓
    Active voice throughout ("We sent" not "A code was sent")
  • ✓
    One idea per sentence, one action per screen
  • ✓
    Currency amounts are specific (₦1,200, not "some money")
Tone & Emotion
  • ✓
    Error messages don't blame the user
  • ✓
    Every error has exactly one actionable next step
  • ✓
    Over-budget states are framed as information, not failure
  • ✓
    Celebration copy matches the size of the win (don't overdo small things)
  • ✓
    No shame, lectures, or condescension anywhere
Cultural Fit
  • ✓
    Copy references local realities (MPESA, okada, canteen, etc.) where appropriate
  • ✓
    No idioms that don't translate across African markets
  • ✓
    Currency format is consistent and market-appropriate
  • ✓
    "Sending money home" is acknowledged as a normal, non-shameful expense
  • ✓
    Income fields accommodate irregular/freelance pay cycles
Completeness
  • ✓
    Every screen has a headline, body (if needed), and CTA
  • ✓
    All empty states have copy and an action button
  • ✓
    All error states are covered, including network/timeout
  • ✓
    Button labels are verbs, not nouns ("Log expense" not "Expense Log")
  • ✓
    Skip/cancel paths exist and have copy
Accessibility
  • ✓
    Icon-only buttons have descriptive ARIA labels
  • ✓
    Color is never the only way information is conveyed
  • ✓
    Reading level accessible to a general 18–25 year old audience
  • ✓
    Form helper text is visible (not just placeholder) for required fields
Catalog Complete
This catalog was written by Maha Noor for Pesa — a student budgeting app for Sub-Saharan Africa. Every word was chosen to be accessible, culturally resonant, and financially empowering for the next generation of African students managing their own money.
